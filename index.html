The National Basketball Association (NBA), comprised of 30 teams (29 in the U.S. and 1 in Canada), stands as the foremost basketball league globally. Originating in 1946 as the Basketball Association of America (BAA), it underwent significant transformations, merging with the National Basketball League (NBL) in 1949 and later integrating with the American Basketball Association (ABA) in 1976, leading to the addition of four franchises. The league's annual calendar unfolds with a regular season spanning from October to April, featuring a rigorous schedule of 82 games per team. This intense competition sets the stage for a captivating playoff tournament that extends into June, capturing the attention of basketball enthusiasts worldwide. In the financial realm, NBA players have achieved global recognition as the highest-paid athletes, securing the top spot in average annual salary as of 2020. This financial dominance underscores the league's position as a powerhouse in the sports industry. Recognized as a major professional sports league in both the United States and Canada, the NBA holds a distinguished status, ranking third in revenue among professional sports leagues in North America, and among the top four globally. This remarkable standing is a testament to the league's widespread popularity and economic influence. Operational and administrative functions are centered around the league's headquarters in Midtown Manhattan, overseeing international and team offices. Additionally, the NBA Entertainment and NBA TV studios, integral components of the league's multimedia presence, are directed out of offices located in Secaucus, New Jersey. Lastly, the NBA's rich history is punctuated by iconic franchises, with the Boston Celtics and the Los Angeles Lakers sharing the record for the most NBA championships, each boasting an impressive 17 titles. In the most recent chapter of the league's narrative, the Denver Nuggets emerged as the reigning champions in the 2023 NBA Finals, triumphing over the Miami Heat.
